# Kajabi Design Framework

These five principles define how we build at Kajabi. They guide our overall approach to craft and quality.

## 1. The 80/20 Model

Not all work deserves the same level of investment. Everything falls into one of two buckets:

**The 80% (System Patterns):** List views, forms, settings, edit pages. The predictable, repeatable backbone. Like an index fund - boring, reliable, compounds over time. Developers should be able to read a pitch and start building immediately using system patterns. Designers polish rather than redesign from scratch.

**The 20% (Magic Experiences):** Onboarding, launching a product, making a first sale, going live with members. These are the moments Heroes remember. This is where we put 80% of our craft. If it doesn't feel right, it doesn't ship.

**How to apply:**
- Before any project, ask: Is this 80% or 20%?
- If 80%: reach for existing patterns, move fast, aim for consistency
- If 20%: slow down, sweat the details, this is where we earn trust

## 2. Get to the Truth Faster

Abstract discussions create false alignment - everyone nods while imagining completely different things. Concrete work reveals the truth.

**The moment something becomes real:**
- Assumptions collapse
- Hidden disagreements surface
- Feasibility becomes obvious
- Scope becomes clear

**How to apply:**
- Don't stop at mockups - build small, complete slices of working code
- Mockups are guesses; working software is truth
- Don't spend time crafting decks or debating abstractions
- Build something real, get feedback, iterate

## 3. Make Complexity Feel Simple

Kajabi is inherently complex under the hood. But complexity isn't the enemy - confusion is. Our job is to absorb complexity so Heroes don't have to deal with it.

**The Slack example:** Their push notification logic involves presence detection, device fallbacks, notification rules, override logic, timing heuristics. Users experience none of it - they just think "it notifies me at the right time on the right device."

**How to apply:**
- Ask: Are we doing the hard work, or pushing it onto Heroes?
- Hide the complexity, expose the clarity
- Make powerful features feel effortless

## 4. Build Taste Through Practice

Taste allows us to stand out and make good judgment calls without needing a rule for every situation. There's no shortcut - taste comes from practice.

**How to apply:**
- Study products that feel great: Linear, Raycast, Notion, Apple, Airbnb, Ramp
- When something delights or frustrates you, pause and ask why
- What did they do? What did they choose not to do? Where does the clarity come from?
- Write it down, share it with the team - this builds shared standards

## 5. Live the Product

We build perspective by living in the product alongside our Heroes. This means: create a site, build products and offers, send broadcasts, run automations, explore the admin intentionally.

**How to apply:**
- Feel what Heroes feel on a day-to-day basis
- See the inconsistencies, notice what feels good, hard, or broken
- Stay slightly discontent about the experience - not discouraged, but energized
- Ask: "This is good... but how could it be great?"

---

## The Through Line

These ideas connect:
- **80/20** tells you where to invest your effort
- **Get to truth faster** keeps you honest about what you're building
- **Make complexity simple** is the craft itself
- **Build taste** sharpens your judgment
- **Live the product** grounds your taste in reality
